Final Project: Research

Inspiration

By Kevin Lee

This project was completed by my friend as a part of a project at keio University's School of Media Design. It explored the alternative third person pronoun in Chinese as a provocation and an investigation into the inherently non-binary nature of Chinese."By encouraging people who natively read Chinese characters to deeper question their formation, and the implications it has for Chinese / Japanese societies, it hopes to reveal a flexibility and playfulness built into one of the worlds oldest languages. Binary gender pronouns came into existence in Chinese after the introduction of the female pronoun, which, in a stark contrast to the West, fueled a gendered conciousness and an empowerment of women within China. 祂 and 㐴 (visually similar to "TA", netspeak for gender neutral 他) are presented as new pronouns to highlight the inherent gender fluidity within the Chinese writing system."

Values: Investigates his cultural background; linguisistcs; reflects ideals of society

By Jiaqi Zhang

"1000 most common Chinese words were written in random order, which are supposed not to make any sense. The installation is activated by a translation app. Based on camera capture and image recognition, the machine translation provides a real-time "non-sense" "poem"."

Values: Based on Chinese culture; incorporates technological interaction

Topic of Interest

As someone who was born in China and then immigrated to the United States at a young age, I feel equal presence of both cultures in my life. Drawing upon my own experiences, I'm interested in investigating the 1.5 generation immigrant(someone who immigrated at a young age) experience.
