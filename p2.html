Instruction Sets for Strangers

[Prototype 1]

Process

We decided to use Idea 2 from the brainstorming phase as a basis for this prototype. In order to give people a different view of the fountain, we used a sheet of yellow and blue acetate to creat a green filter effect when combined together. In the spirit of rapid prototyping and sustainability, we salvaged cardboard from recycling bins to make the filter box and stand.

Site Testing

Interactions

Looking, photo taking, asking

Observations/Findings

  • Some people were curious and asked us about it when we were setting up
  • People are reluctant to touch it (possibly because it looks flimsy)
  • Several people look through our filter, but soon walked away
  • It fell over multiple time from the wind.

Insights & Improvements

  • The affordance for the removable filter needs to be more clear. Adding arrow tabs to the side of the filters can help signal the sliding action.
  • The base of the stand needs to be wider and heavier to withstand the wind. A tripod can be a good alternative.
  • There can be a wider variety of filters to make the interaction more engaging.
  • It wasn't clear that people can approach the project. We can add a dot or footprint in front of it to make it more inviting.
